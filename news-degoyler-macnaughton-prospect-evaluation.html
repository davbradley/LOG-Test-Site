DeGoyler MacNaughton Conduct Prospect Evaluation

  • October 1, 2014

Lockhart has hired DeGolyer and MacNaughton, a leading independent consulting firm focused on the petroleum industry, to complete a prospect evaluation on the Raton Basin Play. DeGolyer and MacNaughton has been providing petroleum consulting services throughout the world since 1936. The firm's professional engineers, geologists, geophysicists, petrophysicists, and economists are engaged in the independent appraisal of oil and gas properties, evaluation of hydrocarbon and other mineral prospects, basin evaluations, comprehensive field studies, equity studies, and studies of supply and economics related to the energy industry. DeGolyer and MacNaughton is an industry leader in the evaluation of prospective resources. The firm’s experience is recognized by large international exploration firms, national oil companies, and financial institutions.

A preliminary report has been completed and a final report will be available first quarter 2015.

The play holds potential for over 8 trillion cubic feet of gas.

“This report presents estimates, as of October 1, 2014, of the conventional and unconventional prospective petroleum resources of various prospects located in Colfax County, New Mexico.... The conventional and unconventional prospective resources estimates presented in this report have been prepared in accordance with the Petroleum Resources Management System (PRMS) approved in March 2007 by the Society of Petroleum Engineers, the World Petroleum Council, the American Association of Petroleum Geologists, and the Society of Petroleum Evaluation Engineers.”

-DeGolyer and MacNaughton report for Lockhart Oil and Gas, October 1, 2014.

The report evaluates the Conventional and Unconventional Prospective Resources of 15 prospects owned by Lockhart Oil and Gas.

Unconventional Targets

  • Carlyle Shale
  • Granerous Shale

Two of the Unconventional prospects evaluated, the Niobrara and the Pierre Shale, have remarkable thicknesses. The preliminary report shows the Niobrara Shale to have a mean average of 670 feet thickness and the Pierre Shale to have a mean average of 1,850 feet.
