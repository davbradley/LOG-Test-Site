Raton Basin Geological Information

The Raton Basin spans portions of southern Colorado and northern New Mexico. It is a is a geologic structural basin and its name is derived from the Raton Pass and the nearby town of Raton, New Mexico. The area of the basin spans approximately 50 miles (80 km) east-west, and 90 miles (140 km) north-south. It is located in the counties of Las Animas and Huerfano in Colorado and in New Mexico, the county of Colfax. The basin forms the foothills of the Rocky Mountains and runs east of the Sangre de Cristo Range.
