Lockhart O&G Acquires Raton Basin

  • January 28, 2015

We are pleased to announce our newest acquisition in the Raton Basin. This parcel contains a leasing opportunity for ~204,000 (gross), 193,000 (net) acres of virtually untapped reserves situated in the Raton Basin in the State of New Mexico. Areas to the north and west in the basin are a source of Coalbed methane. It was named in 2007 by the US Energy Information Administration as the 17th largest source of Natural Gas in the United States. One E&P Company owns ~605,000 net acres in the Western Raton Basin (Vermejo Ranch) which holds 320Bcf of proved reserves and ~76 MMcfe/d of net production out of the Raton and Vermejo coalbeds. These assets have a shallow production decline rate, low operating expenses and more than 1,000 potential future drilling locations. This play utilizes the Vermejo and Raton coals, which are also present in the Raton Basin play.

In the Northern Raton Basin in Colorado, another E&P Company has assets that include ~2300 wells on approximately ~198,000 acres. This play also has Vermejo and Raton coals present as well as Pierre shale, which in 2008 was identified as a shale-gas target. Pierre shale is present in the Raton Basin play as well.

The findings of the evaluation illustrate the trillions of cubic feet of natural gas available on the acreage but it does not include the potentially huge oil reserves. A number of wells that have been drilled across the area have experienced a large number of shows of oil and gas from several different formations. The shallowest formations that are present across the acreage with shows are the Pierre Shale and the Niobrara Shale.

A number of wells that have been drilled across the area have experienced a large number of shows of oil and gas from several different formations.

The Niobrara and the Pierre Shales have remarkable thicknesses. The preliminary report shows the Niobrara Shale to have a mean average of 670 feet thickness and the Pierre Shale to have a mean average thickness of 1,850 feet. The Niobrara Shale is productive along strike from the prospect acreage going north into Colorado where several new fields have been discovered in the Niobrara Shale. Research papers on the Raton Basin show both the Pierre and the Niobrara shales to have good TOC numbers and are located across the prospect acreage in the Oil Window. The majority of the wells drilled that had shows from the Niobrara shale were older drilled wells that predate the more recent shale plays. This acreage of the Raton Basin has only had exploratory wells drilled on it to date, making it an enterprise building opportunity in the oil and gas market.
